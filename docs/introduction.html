Introducción Análisis Modelos

Clasificación de Melanoma y otras lesiones en la piel utilizando redes neuronales

Objective

Skin cancer is the most common form of cancer in the United States. Skin cancer screenings are often performed using a dermatoscope, allowing visualization of pigmented structure of skin lesions that are not visiable to the naked eye. Recent development of mobile dermatoscope, which can easily attach to a smartphone or tablet, opens new opportunities for the automation of skin cancer detection and prevention. This study is an initiative step into automating skin cancer diagnosis by using convolutional neural networks to build a classifier to detect different types of skin lesions.

Dataset

Skin Cancer MNIST:HAM10000 consists of 10015 dermatoscopic images of pigmented lesions collected from multiple sources. The images are classified into 7 categories: melanocytic nevi, melanoma, benign keratosis-like lesions, basal cell carcinoma, actinic keratoses, vascular lesions and dermatofibroma. Benign keratosis-like lesions, vascular lesions, dermatofibroma and majority of melanocytic nevi are noncancerous. Actinic keratosis is a pre-cancer skin condition. Melanoma and basal cell carcinoma are two forms of skin cancer.

Technologies

Python 3.6, Pytorch 1.0 and common python packages (pandas, numpy, etc.) are used to build the models. The models were trained on an AWS EC2 P3 instance with 1 Tesla V100 GPU core and 16GB of GPU memory.

Bootstrap 4 and d3.js are used to build the front end.
